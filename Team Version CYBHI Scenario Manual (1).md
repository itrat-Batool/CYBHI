**Modifier Explanations (Quick Reference)**

- **U4** = CYBHI program identifier (required on all claims).
- **HA** = Individual service (psychoeducation/health behavior intervention).
- **HQ** = Group service (psychoeducation/health behavior intervention).
- **U2** = Required for CHW self-management education codes (98960–98962).
- **U1** = Dyadic service (caregiver + child).
- **93** = Telehealth via audio-only.
- **2195** = Telehealth via audio + video.

**Scenario:** H2014 – Skills Training & Development (Individual, 15 min)

**Raw Incident Report**

“This is Officer Ramirez. It’s 3rd period in the east hallway. Jordan Martinez, 9th grade, was yelling at another student, slammed his backpack against the wall, refused to go back to class. He was by pacing, fists clenched, looked really upset. No one got hurt, but it was loud and disruptive. I walked him down to the office so he could cool off. He said the other kid keeps making fun of him and he’s tired of it.”

**Submitted SOAP Note (Report Format)**

**Student Name:** Jordan Martinez

**Grade:** 9

**Date/Time:** November 20, 2025 – 3rd Period

**Location:** East Hallway

**S (Subjective):** Student stated, _“He keeps making fun of me in front of everyone. I can’t take it anymore.”_

**O (Objective):** Student was pacing, yelling, fists clenched. Backpack slammed against wall. No injuries observed.

**A (Assessment):** Peer conflict escalating to anger response. Risk of further disruption if not addressed.

**P (Plan):** Immediate referral to counselor for anger management skills training. Follow-up session scheduled for next week.

## **Raw Counseling Report (Counselor Narrative Input)**

“This is the counselor documenting the 30‑minute session with Jordan Martinez. When he arrived, he was visibly tense—pacing slightly, breathing heavily, and avoiding eye contact. He reported feeling embarrassed and angry about being teased and said he reacted without thinking. I introduced basic anger management strategies and coached him through deep breathing and counting exercises. We then practiced using ‘I’ statements through role‑play, and I helped him identify specific triggers and alternative responses. Jordan was hesitant at first but gradually engaged more fully. By the end of the session, he appeared calmer and was able to restate a peer’s comment without escalating.”

**Counselor Service Notes**

- **Session Length:** 30 minutes
- **Interventions:**
    - Introduced anger management strategies (deep breathing, counting to ten).
    - Role-played conflict resolution using “I” statements.
    - Helped Jordan identify triggers and alternative responses.
- **Observations:** Jordan was initially resistant but engaged in role-play. Demonstrated ability to restate peer’s comments without escalation.
- **Clinical Impression**: Students benefited from structured skills training. Needs reinforcement in future sessions.

**Resolution**

Jordan agreed to practice breathing techniques when frustrated and to meet with a counselor again next week. Teacher notified of plan.

**Claim Code**

**H2014-HA-U4**

- **HA = Individual psychoeducation (required for one-on-one skills training).**
- **U4 = CYBHI program identifier (required on all claims).**

**Billing Calculation:**

- **30 minutes ÷ 15 = 2 units**
- **Rate = $20.11 per unit**
- **Total = 2 × $20.11 = $40.22**
